High-emphasis CTAs are used for calls to action that need to stand out against its surroundings, or for the primary action when multiple calls to action are present. They can be styled by including both the cta and cta--high classes.

Medium-emphasis CTAs are used for calls to action that do not take precedence in a given situation, for instance as a secondary action when multiple calls to action are present. They can be styled by including both the cta and cta--medium classes.

Low-emphasis CTAs are used when the call to action is not meant to stand out but rather be a smaller part of a larger component, for instance on cards. Low-emphasis CTAs must always include an icon to distinguish it as clickable. They can be styled by including bot the cta and cta--low classes.

Internal Link External Link Back Link

A very common use of the low-emphasis CTA across components is as like that points to either an internal or external URL, or as a back indicator. As a convenience, they can be styled by including both the cta and either the cta--link or cta--back classes (respectively), which will consistently handle these use cases, complete with internal vs external arrow indicator.
